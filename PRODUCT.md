# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Shira Fit is used by three roles in a fitness studio context:

- **Athletes** using a phone between daily routines to browse sessions, register/waitlist/cancel, and view their upcoming schedule.
- **Coaches** using mobile during training days to manage their sessions, attendance, participants, and notes with minimal friction.
- **Managers** using the Expo web PWA (and sometimes mobile) to oversee sessions, approvals, finance, receipts, staff tools, and reporting.

## Product Purpose

Provide a clear, fast system to run studio training sessions end-to-end: discovery, registration, waitlist, attendance, cancellations, billing, Israeli digital receipts, and operational management across mobile (primary) and web PWA (secondary, same Expo codebase).

Success looks like fewer missed sessions, fewer admin mistakes, and a calmer “day-of” workflow for staff.

## Brand Personality

Modern, slick, simple.

Voice principles: concise, confident, encouraging. Prefer plain language over corporate phrasing.

## Anti-references

- Cluttered, overly “corporate” dashboards and dense tables everywhere.
- Childish, gaming-style UI patterns.
- Flashy colors, neon accents, and “look at me” styling.
- Excessive animations, gimmicky motion, or decorative effects.

## Design Principles

- **Clarity over density**: make the next action obvious, and hide complexity until it’s needed.
- **Calm confidence**: the UI should feel dependable on busy training days.
- **Mobile-first by default**: prioritize fast scanning, one-handed use, and big touch targets.
- **Respect focus**: keep copy short, avoid visual noise, and use motion only to clarify state changes.
- **Consistency beats cleverness**: predictable patterns across athlete/coach/manager flows.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- **WCAG**: aim for **AA**.
- **RTL**: full RTL support across mobile and web.
- **Contrast & type**: high contrast and readable typography; avoid low-contrast placeholder-as-label patterns.
- **Touch targets**: large touch targets on mobile; spacing that supports tap accuracy.
- **Reduced motion**: support reduced motion preferences where possible; avoid motion-only meaning.
- **Responsive**: mobile-first responsive behavior (including manager web views on smaller screens).
